Warm-Up Protocol

Complete before every session. Do not skip — cold muscles underperform and injury risk increases significantly.

Asthma: Have your inhaler accessible before starting. 2 puffs of reliever inhaler 10–15 min before exercise helps prevent symptoms.

1. General Warm-Up (5 min)

Light Cardio
5 min
Brisk treadmill walk, cross-trainer or bike on low resistance. Raise heart rate gently — warm but not breathless.

2. Injury Rehab & Activation (8–10 min)

Hip Flexor Stretch
2 × 30s each
Lunge position, front knee at 90°, drive hips forward. Important for anyone sitting at a desk — tight hip flexors pull on the lower back.
Band Pull-Aparts
2 × 15
Light band at chest height. Pull apart to full extension. Activates rear delts and rotator cuff — improves shoulder mechanics and reduces elbow stress during pressing.
Bodyweight Squat
2 × 10
Full depth. Controlled descent. Reinforces lower body movement pattern before adding load.
Arm Circles
2 × 10 each direction
Large controlled circles. Shoulder mobility before pressing and pulling.
Wrist Extensor Stretch
2 × 30s each
Arm straight, palm facing down. Gently pull fingers toward you with other hand. Hold 30s. Directly stretches the overloaded tendon in tennis elbow. Do this daily.
Reverse Wrist Curl (Light DB)
2 × 15
Palm facing down, light dumbbell. Curl wrist up, lower slowly. Strengthens wrist extensors — the root cause of tennis elbow. 1–2kg only to start.

3. Daily Rehab (do these every day, not just training days)

Child's Pose
1 × 60s
Kneel, sit back onto heels, reach arms forward on the floor. Hold and breathe. Decompresses the lower spine — do this morning and evening if possible.
Deep Glute Stretch (Pigeon Pose)
1 × 30s each side
Brings knee to same-side wrist, extend back leg. Deep hip flexor and glute stretch. Directly addresses hip tightness contributing to lower back pain.
