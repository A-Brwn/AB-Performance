Your Overhead Press 1RM
How to test your 1RM:
Warm up thoroughly — 5 min cardio, then 3–4 progressively heavier warm-up sets (50% × 8, 65% × 5, 80% × 3, 90% × 1). Then attempt your max. Rest 3–5 minutes between heavy attempts. If you can't test a true 1RM, use the Epley estimator below — take a weight you can lift for 3–6 reps at max effort and enter it.

Warm-Up Protocol

Follow this warm-up before every session. Use the same warm-up on test day. Do not skip — cold muscles underperform and carry higher injury risk.

How to read tempo (e.g. 3–1–1): First number = eccentric (lowering) in seconds  ·  Second = pause at bottom  ·  Third = concentric (lifting) in seconds  ·  X = as fast/explosive as possible. Not every exercise has a prescribed tempo — where none is listed, use a natural controlled pace.

Wall Slide
2 × 10

Overhead Tricep Stretch
1 × 45s each arm
Reach one arm overhead, bend elbow, use other hand to press elbow back gently. Tight tricep long head limits overhead lockout.
Scapular Push-Up
2 × 10
In a plank, keep arms straight and protract/retract shoulder blades only. Activates serratus anterior — keeps shoulder blade flat under overhead load.

4-WEEK 1RM BOOSTER — BARBELL OVERHEAD PRESS

Duration
4 Weeks
Sessions/Week
3
Primary Muscles
Shoulders, Triceps, Upper Chest, Core
Key Cue
Rib cage down, vertical bar path, full lockout

This programme can be run alongside your existing training. Aim for 3 sessions per week with at least 48 hours rest between sessions working the same muscle groups.

Structure: Week 1 Accumulation (70–75%) → Week 2 Intensification (75–82%) → Week 3 Peaking (82–90%) → Week 4 Deload & 1RM Test.

Strength gains over 4 weeks are primarily neural — your nervous system learns to recruit more motor units and fire them harder and faster. The peaking structure maximises this adaptation.
