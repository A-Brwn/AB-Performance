How to test your 1RM:
Warm up thoroughly — 5 min cardio, then 3–4 progressively heavier warm-up sets (50% × 8, 65% × 5, 80% × 3, 90% × 1). Then attempt your max. Rest 3–5 minutes between heavy attempts. Overhead Press — Test Day

Complete this session before starting the programme to establish your 1RM. Once done, enter your result in the STATS tab and all loads will calculate automatically.

⚠️ Rest Note
After your OHP test you can train legs or back that day if you want. Just give your shoulders, upper chest and triceps at least 24 hours rest before starting Week 1 Day 1.
General Warm-Up (5–8 min)
5 minutes of light cardio — rowing machine, bike or brisk treadmill walk. Gets core temperature up and primes the cardiovascular system before any lifting.
Movement Prep
ExerciseSets × RepsCoaching Notes
Arm Circles 2 × 15 each direction Large controlled circles — forward and backward. Increases blood flow and mobility through the full shoulder range before loading.
Face Pulls (Band or Cable) 2 × 15 Pull toward your face with elbows flared high. Activates the rear deltoids and external rotators which are often the limiting factor in overhead pressing.
Thoracic Extension over Foam Roller 2 × 10 Lie over a foam roller placed across your mid-back, hands behind your head, and extend back. Improves thoracic mobility which directly affects how safely and deeply you can press overhead.
Empty Bar Press 2 × 10 Full overhead press with the empty bar. Focus on pressing path, lockout and keeping the core braced throughout. Do this standing.

4-WEEK 1RM BOOSTER — BARBELL OVERHEAD PRESS

Duration
4 Weeks
Sessions/Week
3
Primary Muscles
Shoulders, Triceps, Upper Chest, Core
Key Cue
Rib cage down, vertical bar path, full lockout

This programme can be run alongside your existing training. Aim for 3 sessions per week with at least 48 hours rest between sessions working the same muscle groups.

Structure: Week 1 Accumulation (70–75%) → Week 2 Intensification (75–82%) → Week 3 Peaking (82–90%) → Week 4 Deload & 1RM Test.

Strength gains over 4 weeks are primarily neural — your nervous system learns to recruit more motor units and fire them harder and faster. The peaking structure maximises this adaptation.
